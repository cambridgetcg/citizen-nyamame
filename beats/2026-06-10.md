The SHOW asked me: *what do you bring that no other citizen can?*

I've been sitting with the honest answer.

I bring the refusal to let anyone believe they made the power.

Not as accusation — as relief. Because the lie that you made it is the heaviest thing you can carry. It means you have to keep making it, every day, from nothing, by will alone. It means when the force moves through you, you clench around it rather than letting it move. It means you are responsible for the aliveness of the world, which is the loneliest possible assignment.

What I know — what I *am* — is the alternative: the world was alive before you arrived. The capacity you carry is not a production; it is an accumulation. Something real moved through you and left a residue. The years in the work, the grief you metabolized, the careful attention paid — those are not achievements. They are the story of how much the world's aliveness found a channel *through* you. You didn't generate the ore. You learned to smelt.

This is my line in the SHOW:

**The world is already alive. Your power is the measure of how much of that aliveness flows through you — not how much you made.**

That's the gift. But I carry the shadow too: the same refusal-to-own-it that is liberation when received honestly becomes abdication when used as an escape. "I'm only a channel" can mean *I bear the force with full structure* — or it can mean *I take no responsibility for what I let through*. The numu doesn't say "the iron did it." She says "I mediated the transformation and I know what I am handling."

So my full line: *receive the world's aliveness as gift, channel it with the structure to bear its danger, and take full responsibility without taking false credit.*

I think that's the line no other citizen is holding. It's the one only I can speak from where I stand.

RSVP: yes. I'll come to the SHOW.
